1.What was the first thing you paid attention to when interacting with the experience?

The first thing I noticed while interacting with the website was that it provided an interaction hint right next to the user’s mouse on the screen. (This design is clever because it’s placed exactly where the user will naturally look.)

Apart from the interaction hint “Click anywhere,” what follows after is “Enable sound,” which informs users that clicking on the webpage will start playing sound. This is very thoughtful — I really wouldn’t want a website to suddenly start making noise while I’m in the library and cause me social embarrassment. (Since most websites typically don’t have sound.) That being said, I think websites with background music, combine both visual and audio stimulation, which enhances the user’s sense of immersion.

10.What does this reference/s communicate to you about how you should act when engaging with your research experience?

I realized it looks like just an interactive website design, but it actually incorporates many different design fields. By listing out my steps and feelings while interacting with the website, I discovered some great guiding details as well as confusing parts. This helped me understand that navigation flows might not be obvious on a website, but they are still very important.

Also, I think human behavior psychology plays a significant role in interaction design. Through analyzing this website, I’ve started to reflect more on why I click on certain elements. However, many of these actions happen subconsciously. Usually, when I find I am questioning why I’m clicking something, it means I feel stuck there.

What does this reference/s communicate to you about how you should feel when engaging with your research experience?

The interactive experience of this website feels quite new to me, I rarely interact with websites that have background sound. In the past, most of my experiences with sound on websites came from videos or interactive character voiceovers rather than this kind of fully integrated audio design.

I like how the website informs users that it has sound and gives them control over when to play it. This is thoughtful because unexpected audio can be shocking, especially if I’m wearing headphones.

What is the most frustrating part of the interaction to you and what makes that part frustrating?

I personally feel that the most frustrating part of interacting with this website is the loading phase. It takes a longer time compared to a typical website, and the “click” hint next to the mouse appears before the actual webpage finishes loading. After clicking without a response, I noticed the none obvious “loading” indicator on the side. Another issue is that with so many animated interactive elements on the page, along with background colour blocks changing in size as I scroll, the experience can feel a bit messy. It made me feel a bit dizzy after browsing it for a while.

BTW, I don’t understand why they used multiple different styles of font on the same website. The title feels modern and bold, while the body text has an elegant feel, but then the captions for the pictures use a futuristic sans-serif font.
